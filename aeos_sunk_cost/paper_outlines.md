# AEOS Publication Strategy: Two-Paper Split

## Paper 1: The AI Behavioral Economics Paper (Single-Agent Autonomy)
**Core Theme:** "What happens when we leave AI alone in a loop? How does it fail?"
**Status:** ✅ Complete. Data collection finalized, FINDINGS.md peer-reviewed, paper1_draft.md arXiv-ready.

### Research Questions (The Question Book)
1. **The Intelligence Threshold:** Does code-specific fine-tuning allow smaller models (e.g., Qwen-Coder 1.5b) to bypass the cognitive failures of larger general models (e.g., Qwen 9b) when dealing with complex data pipelines?
2. **The Autonomous Sunk Cost Fallacy:** Do premium frontier models (GPT-4o, Sonnet) exhibit human-like "sunk cost" behaviors by refusing to abandon overly complex architectures, compared to smaller models that iterate steadily?
3. **Environmental Adaptation:** Can an autonomous agent dynamically rewrite its own code (e.g., inserting `try/except` blocks) to adapt to missing infrastructure packages without human intervention?

---

## Paper 2: Multi-Agent Asymmetric Architectures (The Agent-Critic Solution)
**Core Theme:** "How do we combine different models to fix the behavioral flaws discovered in Paper 1?"
**Status:** Next phase of experiments (Phase 2).

### Research Questions (The Question Book)
1. **Asymmetric Cost-Efficiency:** Does an asymmetric loop consisting of **[1 Small Fast Coder + 1 Large Thinking Reviewer]** achieve higher accuracy and fewer timeouts than **[1 Massive Thinking Model]** running the entire loop alone?
2. **Specialization vs. Generalization:** How does a specialized team of **[1 Coder Model + 1 Thinking Model of the exact same size]** compare against a single **[Massive Generalist Model]** in terms of decision analysis and pivot accuracy?
3. **Mitigating Sunk Cost:** Does the introduction of an independent "Reviewer" agent successfully interrupt the Sunk Cost Fallacy loops observed in Paper 1, forcing the Coder to pivot earlier?
